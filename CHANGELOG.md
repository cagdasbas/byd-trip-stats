## [2.12.0] - 2026-Jul-11

> **What's new in a nutshell**
>
> A big customisation-and-looks release, built around a brand-new **Cards dashboard layout (Pro)** — an alternative to the classic chart-centric dashboard that trades the large central graph for **big, well-spaced tiles with much larger fonts**. The top **power row** (Power / Speed / SoC / Range / Distance) and the bottom **Trip Tracking** bar stay put; the **two middle rows are fully yours** — tap the **pencil** in the top-right to enter edit mode, where every tile gently **wobbles** and you can **drag to reorder** them and **show/hide** the ones you don't care about, with the rest **growing to fill the freed space**. The range-projection **chart sits pinned in the centre**, spanning both rows, and **expands to a full-size window on tap** (or from the Range tile); hide it and the cards reclaim the whole width. Even the five **power tiles are reorderable** (they're always visible), and they gained **live subtitles** — Power reads *Idle / Accelerating / Decelerating / Charging*, Speed reads *Parked / Driving*. ## [2.11.0] - 2026-Jul-05

> **What's new in a nutshell**
>
> The charging detail view gains a **Cell V** tab showing each cell's high and low voltage across a session — so you can watch the pack's voltage climb as it fills and see how tightly the cells stay balanced (the high−low spread). And every charging chart — Power, Voltage, Cell V and Temperature — can now be plotted against **time or state of charge** with a Time / SoC toggle.
>
> On the driving side, the **live range-projection curve** is far steadier and better-bounded. The wild up-and-down on hilly motorways — a long descent sending the projection shooting up, the next climb dropping it — is reined in by an **optimism cap** (it can no longer claim you're driving dramatically better than your own trip average) plus source-level smoothing, and the violent spikes in the **first kilometre** of a drive are gone (the estimate now starts from your lifetime average and eases onto the trip's own rate). It also no longer collapses to zero on a stray reading, and reopening an in-progress **plug-in-hybrid** trip no longer overstates its EV range. It's still a *live* estimate — it tracks how you're driving, so it moves with the road — but it should feel much closer to a trip computer now, without the fantasy swings. And a trip's **mode insights** now always compare the drive (and regen) modes you actually used, letting the numbers decide which was more efficient rather than assuming Eco always beats Sport.
>
> The whole app is now **multilingual** — every screen is fully translated into **27 languages** (28 including English), pick yours under Settings → Language, and it overrides the head unit's own language for the app only.

### Added

- **Multilingual support — the app is fully translated into 27 languages** — every screen, dialog, chart label and FAQ entry now has a complete translation; there's no hardcoded English left in the UI. Choose your language under **Settings → App → Language** (it overrides the car's system language for BYD Trip Stats only, and *System default* follows the head unit). Bundled languages: Greek, German, French, Spanish, Italian, Dutch, Polish, Czech, Hungarian, Romanian, Swedish, Finnish, Danish, Norwegian, Portuguese, Brazilian Portuguese, Russian, Turkish, Thai, Hindi, Hebrew, Indonesian, Vietnamese, Japanese, Korean, and Simplified &amp; Traditional Chinese. Product terms (mode names like Eco / Normal / Sport, ABRP, MQTT, Pro) are kept in their original form across every language. Hebrew is right-to-left; the layout mirrors accordingly.
- **"Cell V" tab in Charging Detail** — a new tab plotting the **highest and lowest cell voltage** across a charging session, with the high−low **spread** (cell imbalance) shown in mV on the crosshair. Like the Temperature tab, each line starts where the BMS actually begins reporting the per-cell range — some firmwares only expose it partway through a charge — and the axis scales to the real ~3 V window. Handy for spotting a weak cell or watching the pack top-balance near full. Built on cell-voltage data the app already records, so it works for past sessions too.
- **Time / SoC x-axis on the charging charts** — the **Voltage**, **Cell V** and **Temperature** charts gain the same **Time | SoC** toggle the Power chart already had, so any of them can be plotted against elapsed time or against state of charge, and the choice is **shared across all four charts** (switch one to SoC and they all follow) — handy for lining a voltage or temperature curve up to a given SoC. In time mode each chart now also overlays the **SoC** curve on a right axis (as the Power chart did), so SoC context is always visible. The old **"Power + SoC"** tab is renamed **"Power"** now that SoC is selectable everywhere.

### Changed

- **Consistent branded top bar on every screen, and screenshot-anywhere** — every screen now shows the BYD logo + "trip stats" wordmark at the far left of its top bar (previously only the dashboard had it), sized to match the dashboard exactly. The back/close button now sits **after** the logo rather than sharing its corner, so a mistimed tap can no longer land on the logo instead of Back. Tapping the logo saves a screenshot (Pro) from **any** screen now, not just the dashboard.
- **Trip History / Charging History subtitle moved below the title** — the small explanatory hint under "Trip History" / "Charging History" now sits on its own line beneath the title, matching Seasonal Analysis, Tags and Routes, instead of being squeezed beside it.
- **Internal: large UI screens split into focused per-tab / per-component modules** — charging detail, route analysis, heatmaps, dashboard, settings, trip detail / compare / history and battery degradation were each broken out of their monolithic files into smaller modules, and all four charging charts (Power, Voltage, Cell V, Temperature) were consolidated onto one shared chart engine (which is what let the Time / SoC toggle and SoC overlay be single-place changes). No visible behaviour change; purely maintainability.
- **Drive- and regen-mode insights now always compare, and never assume which mode "should" win** — the Mode Insights card on a trip's route analysis previously only spoke up for a few hardcoded cases (Eco vs Normal being equal, Sport costing more than Normal, High regen not helping), and stayed **silent** the rest of the time — including when you drove **Eco and Sport with no Normal in between** (no comparison appeared at all), or whenever the outcome ran against the textbook expectation. That last part mattered: you can drive Eco with your foot down and Sport gently, so Eco isn't *always* the more efficient run. The insight is now **symmetric and driven purely by what was measured** — whenever a trip used **two different drive (or regen) modes** each over a meaningful distance, it always reports a result, naming whichever mode the data shows was actually more efficient (or better at recovering energy), by how much, or that the two were about the same. No mode is assumed better than another; the numbers decide. When a trip used all three drive modes, it compares the two you drove the most.
- **Range projection: a smarter cold start, and an honest model badge** — in the first moments of a drive, before the trip itself has gathered enough data to estimate consumption, the projection now uses **your own lifetime average consumption** across all past trips (once you've logged at least ~50 km of history) instead of the car's fixed catalog rate — a far more realistic starting point that reflects how *you* actually drive. As the trip builds up its own data the projection moves on to this-trip and recent-driving estimates exactly as before. The projection-model badge that used to read **"● Speed bins"** is now labelled **"● Trip average"**, which is what that tier actually shows — this trip's running average consumption — so the badge no longer implies a per-speed-band model it isn't using.

### Fixed

- **Charging chart y-axis labels collapsed to whole numbers on a flat session** — e.g. a battery-voltage or temperature chart that barely moves gave an axis reading just two or three repeated whole numbers. Tick labels now use enough decimal places for the zoom level — "3.90, 3.92, …" — across all the charging charts.
- **Charge power now reports the accurate, decimal figure during AC charging** — the charge power on the dashboard, in the recorded session curve, and on the MQTT `charging_power` sensor is now taken from the instrument cluster's own charge-power reading — the same value the BYD app shows (e.g. 6.1 kW) — on cars that expose it. On cars whose dedicated charging sensor under-reports (an Atto 3 showed a flat ~2 kW for a real ~4 kW charge, wall socket confirmed 4.6 kW), or for a DC charge begun with the car switched on where that sensor can stay silent, it falls back to the battery pack's own power reading. Energy totals are unchanged (those come from the State-of-Charge gain); pure-EV only.
- **Projected-range curve swung wildly on hilly routes, spiked in the first kilometre, and could reach physically-impossible figures downhill** — several causes, fixed together so the line is much steadier:
    - **Downhill balloons are now bounded.** A long descent drives your *measured* consumption toward zero, which ballooned the projected range (reported shooting past 500 km before snapping back on the next climb). An **optimism cap** now bounds it — the projection can't claim you're driving more than ~1.5× better than your own demonstrated trip average. It's one-sided on purpose: a climb reading *worse* than your average still shows its shorter range honestly, so range is never over-stated toward stranding you.
    - **The rate no longer jitters.** Consumption was read from just the two ends of the rolling window, so the battery meter's coarse steps made it wobble; it's now a **least-squares fit across the whole window**, and the window's energy is built from clamped per-sample steps so a mid-trip meter re-baseline or a burst of regen can't inject a false spike.
    - **The start of a drive is calm.** Dividing energy by a near-zero distance is numerically wild, so the first few hundred metres used to spike between extremes. The estimate now **starts from your lifetime average and eases onto the trip's own rate** over the first 3 km.
    - **No more step at 3 km.** The hand-off from the "trip average" model to the "recent driving" model was an instant jump; it's now a smooth crossfade.

    The projection is still a live estimate that tracks how you actually drive — but without the fantasy swings. The same fixes apply to a reopened trip's rebuilt curve, so it still matches the live one.
- **Last distance label on the range-projection chart was clipped** — the right-most x-axis value (e.g. "12.6") had its trailing digit cut off at the chart's edge because it was centre-anchored on the boundary. The first and last labels are now edge-anchored so they stay fully on-screen.
- **Projected range could briefly collapse to zero (and stay scarred)** — a single stray telemetry packet where state-of-charge momentarily read 0 made the projection compute a 0 km range, drop the curve to the floor and back, and — worse — bake that spike into the saved curve so it survived every reopen of the trip. Such a reading is now treated as "no data for this instant": the line simply leaves a gap instead of diving to zero.
- **Reopening an in-progress plug-in-hybrid trip could overstate its EV range** — when a PHEV trip was rebuilt from storage (reopening the app mid-drive, or after the background service restarted), it estimated remaining EV energy from battery-capacity × state-of-charge, while the **live** view used the BMS's own remaining-EV-energy reading — which correctly sets aside the charge the car reserves for hybrid operation. So a reopened curve could project more EV km than the live one near the bottom of the charge. The app now records that BMS reading with every data point (a one-time database upgrade) and the rebuilt curve uses it, so reopening a trip matches the live projection. Pure-EV cars are unaffected (they never hit this path), and trips recorded before the upgrade fall back to the old estimate exactly as before.

## [2.10.0] - 2026-Jun-27

> **What's new in a nutshell**
>
> A trip-organisation release built around three features that work together. **Trip merging** stitches two trips that were really one journey — split by a brief stop like a petrol station or a red-light timeout — back into a single trip, with honest numbers (distance, energy and driving time are added up from each trip's own figures, so a quirk where the battery's discharge counter resets between trips can't distort the result). **Recurring route detection** automatically spots journeys you drive again and again — your daily commute, say — and gives them a new **Routes** screen where you can compare how efficient each run was. And **trip tagging** lets you label trips with reusable, colour-coded tags like "commute", "motorway" or "errand", filter your history by them, and see a per-tag breakdown of distance and efficiency on a new **Tags** screen. It also fixes a gap where a **DC fast charge started with the car switched on** could go unrecorded — the app now reads the car's own charge-power meter to catch it — and tidies up the charging **Temperature** chart so its axis scales to the real range instead of being squashed by not-yet-reported cell readings. For people running their own MQTT broker, the publisher now also speaks **MQTT over WebSocket (ws/wss)** with an explicit TLS toggle, so the broker can sit behind an HTTP reverse proxy instead of needing a raw MQTT port opened, and it now also publishes the **connected WiFi network name** (the `wifi_ssid` sensor), which had previously always come through blank in Home Assistant. And on the diagnostics side, sharing a **vehicle-compatibility probe** is now one tap — the report uploads to a temporary host and the app shows a **QR code** that opens a pre-filled email with the download link, so you can send it from your phone without Telegram or a file transfer.

### Added

- **Trip merging** — combine two trips that were really the same journey, separated by a brief stop (petrol station, charging stop, a red-light timeout that ended the trip early). Select two trips in History's selection mode and tap **Merge**: the earlier trip survives and absorbs the later one, taking over its data points, route segments and tags. Only contiguous trips can be merged — they must be close together in time and continuous on the odometer — so unrelated trips can't be combined by accident. Crucially the merged trip carries **real** numbers, not timeline-derived ones: distance, energy and driving time are the **sum of each trip's own recorded values** (so the BYD discharge counter resetting between the two trips can't corrupt the combined energy, and parked time in the gap isn't counted as driving), while the **start** of the earlier trip and the **end** of the later trip are kept as the journey's real endpoints. This action can't be undone.
- **Recurring route detection** — the app now automatically groups completed trips that are really the same journey so you can compare their efficiency over time. A new **Routes** screen (toolbar icon in History, next to Seasonal) lists each route you've driven **3 or more times**, showing its average, best and worst efficiency, average duration and a per-trip efficiency trend chart; tap any instance to open that trip. Two trips count as the same route when their **start and end locations match** (within ~250 m) **and** their distances are similar — and direction matters, so your commute **to** work and the drive **home** are tracked as separate routes (their efficiency genuinely differs with elevation, traffic and time of day). All computed on-device from coordinates the app already records; no new data is collected.
- **Trip tagging** — label trips with reusable, **auto-coloured** custom tags (e.g. "commute", "motorway", "errand"). Add or remove tags on a trip's **detail screen**, or **bulk-tag** several trips at once from History's selection mode (alongside Merge / Compare / Delete); tags appear as coloured chips in the History list. **Filter** the history list by one or more tags from the filter sheet, and a dedicated **Tags** screen rolls up each tag's trip count, total distance, total energy and average efficiency — so you can compare categories like "motorway" vs "city" at a glance — with options to rename or delete a tag, or jump straight to its trips. Tags are created once and reused; deleting a tag keeps the trips, and merging two trips combines their tags.
- **Send the vehicle-compatibility probe report by QR code** — sharing a compatibility report no longer needs Telegram, adb, or fishing a saved file off the head unit. Settings → *Vehicle Compatibility* gains an **Email via QR code** button: it uploads the report to a temporary public host (litterbox — auto-deleted after **12 hours**) and shows a **QR code** that opens a pre-filled email to bydtripstats@gmail.com with the one-tap download link already in it. Scan it with your phone, pick your email account and press Send — the head unit has no mail app, so this hands the report off to the device that does. A **Copy link** fallback and the raw URL are shown too, and the privacy notice now spells out that this option uploads the report to a temporary public host. The existing **Telegram** and **Save** (to Download) options are unchanged.
- **MQTT over WebSocket (ws / wss), plus an explicit TLS toggle** — the MQTT publisher previously connected only over raw TCP, which can't be routed through an HTTP reverse proxy. You can now enable **WebSocket** transport (with a configurable path, default `/mqtt`) and **TLS** independently in Settings → Connections → MQTT, so users running their own broker on a home server can expose it through nginx / Traefik / Caddy as `wss://your-host/mqtt` instead of opening a raw MQTT port. The broker must have a WebSocket listener enabled (e.g. Mosquitto's `protocol websockets`); the reverse proxy forwards to it. Plain-TCP setups are unaffected — both toggles default off, and existing TLS-on-8883 configs keep working.

### Fixed

- **A DC fast charge started while the car was switched on wasn't recorded as a charging session** — charging detection leaned on the car's *pushed* BMS charging signals (charge-gun / charger-work state, delivered-capacity), and on this firmware that channel can stall: a DC charge begun with the head unit fully powered left them silent, so the dashboard showed the live charge but no session opened. The app now reads the car's own **charge-power meter** (battery voltage × current) directly — a reading it previously logged but never used, and which the old power path couldn't see because it was capped around the 50 kW AC range — so the charge is both detected and recorded with its true power curve (instead of the 0 kW stored before). As a backstop it also treats a large, sustained power flowing *into* the pack while parked as charging. **AC charging is untouched**, and it's pure-EV only — so a plug-in hybrid charging its pack from the engine in Park is never mistaken for a plug-in charge. Charging while the car is **off** is unchanged — still reconstructed from the State-of-Charge gain on the next wake-up.
- **MQTT entities could get stuck "unavailable" in Home Assistant even while telemetry kept publishing** — the publisher announced itself `online` only once, at connect, and relied on the Last Will to mark it `offline` on an unexpected drop. If a stray `offline` ever landed while the app was actually still connected and publishing — for example a retained will arriving after the broker handed the client ID to a fresh connection — nothing ever flipped availability back to `online`, so Home Assistant showed the device unavailable even though fresh state was arriving on the same broker. The app now re-asserts the retained `online` availability periodically (at most once every 30 s, so it adds no meaningful traffic to the live stream), letting availability self-heal within one cycle. Seen with self-hosted brokers; brokers on a always-reachable host rarely produced the stray `offline` in the first place.
- **WiFi SSID was always empty (blank in Home Assistant and in Settings)** — the `wifi_ssid` field was wired through to MQTT and the Settings "Wi-Fi" row, but the value was never actually read — the telemetry builder hardcoded it to an empty string, so the MQTT payload omitted it entirely. The app now reads the connected network name from the head unit (cached, refreshed at most every 30 s) and publishes it. Note: Android only exposes the SSID when location permission is granted **and** location is switched on; with location off the system reports it as unknown, so the field stays blank by design.
- **Charging "Temperature" chart was squashed, with the Cell min / Cell max lines pinned to 0 °C early in a session** — some BYD battery firmwares report only the *average* cell temperature at first and don't expose the per-cell minimum/maximum until partway through a charge. The chart drew those not-yet-reported readings as 0 °C, which both anchored the two lines to the bottom and pulled the whole temperature axis down to 0 — squashing the real ≈30–40 °C range into a sliver at the top. Each line now starts where its data actually begins, and the axis scales to the temperatures present, so the variation is readable. The average line is unchanged — it was available throughout.

## [2.9.1] - 2026-Jun-20

> **What's new in a nutshell**
>
> A maintenance release focused on accuracy. Plug-in hybrid **range projections are no longer wildly inflated** — the live trip loop now reads the actual selected car instead of assuming a big EV battery — and the **Range** readout no longer briefly folds in the fuel range. The **battery degradation** chart, the "Current SoH" card and the battery-health report now match the dashboard's State of Health to one decimal: no more rounded readings, and no more impossible upward tick where the old and new data met (your existing history is upgraded automatically). And the **Brazilian Real (R$)** joins the electricity-tariff currencies.

### Added

- **Brazilian Real (R$ / BRL)** added to the electricity-tariff currency options (Settings → Preferences → Electricity tariff), alongside €, £, $, A$ and ฿.

### Fixed

- **PHEV projected range wildly too high (saturating at the WLTP cap, and a "Range" readout that jumped between values like 75, 446 and 823 km)** — the projection was being computed with a fallback battery size of ~82.56 kWh and as if the car were a pure EV, because the live trip loop wasn't reading the selected car correctly (it relied on a value that was only populated while a specific screen was observing it, and otherwise stayed empty). On a small-battery plug-in hybrid like the Sealion 6 DM-i that hugely inflated the projection — so it pinned at "≥ WLTP" and the Range figure swung around — while it happened to look right on ~82 kWh electric cars. The trip loop now always reads the real selected car (battery size, PHEV status and rated consumption), so the projection uses the correct usable EV capacity and consumption floor again. The **Range** readout under the chart also now prefers the app's stable projected EV range over the car's raw BMS electric-range field (which on PHEVs can briefly report a combined electric **+ fuel** range). BEVs are unaffected.
- **Battery Degradation showed a rounded SoH (e.g. 97.0%) while the dashboard showed the precise value (97.6%)** — the degradation chart and the battery-health report averaged the rounded *integer* SoH stored per data point, losing the decimal the dashboard reads from the car's precise statistic value. The precise SoH is now recorded in its own column and used for the chart, the "Current SoH" card and the exported report, so they match the dashboard to one decimal. Your existing history is upgraded automatically: a one-time background backfill recovers the precise value that was already saved inside each older data point, so the whole SoH series becomes decimal-accurate — and the line no longer ticks *up* (which SoH should never do) where the old rounded data met the new precise recording.

## [2.9.0] - 2026-Jun-19

> **What's new in a nutshell**
>
> Introduces **BYD Trip Stats Pro** — an optional one-time unlock for premium features, mostly as a 'thank-you' gesture: a **cell imbalance alert**, an exportable **battery health report** (PDF/HTML) for resale or warranty disputes, **dashboard screenshots**, and **backups to an SD card** (which survive a firmware downgrade or factory reset). Pro is unlocked with a one-time per-vehicle code, verified entirely on-device — nothing leaves your car. Everything that was free continues to stay as free. Elsewhere: the background service now defaults to **Always On** so parked telemetry and overnight-charging detection keep working, **Settings → Preferences** is reorganised into clear groups, and the battery degradation chart now **automatically hides the old, under-reported SoH readings** from before v2.4.0 so the trend is accurate. Also fixes the Energy Consumption chart flatlining on longer trips, the Speed/Power charts smoothing away brief peaks, and inflated PHEV range projections.

### Added

- **BYD Trip Stats Pro** (Settings → Preferences → *BYD Trip Stats Pro*) — an optional unlock for premium features via a short, one-time unlock code tied to your vehicle. **Scan the on-screen QR code with your phone to email it in one tap** (the QR opens a message to bydtripstats@gmail.com with your Vehicle ID (hashed/encrypted VIN) already filled in, so you just pick your account and press Send), you will receive instructions on your email on how to pay and later on with the 10-character code you get back — checked **entirely on-device** (no account, no sign-in, nothing leaves your car, works fully offline). Because the code is specific to your car, it can't be shared. Everything already in the app stays free; Pro only gates new premium additions.
- **Battery cell imbalance alert** *(Pro)* — an opt-in notification that fires when the live cell voltage spread (highest cell − lowest cell) stays above a configurable limit (default **50 mV**). It watches the same diagnostic as the Cell Voltage Spread heatmap, so a weak or failing cell can flag itself while you drive or charge rather than only when you go looking. To avoid false alarms it waits for the spread to hold over several consecutive readings, alerts once per episode (re-arming only after the spread recovers), and stays quiet below 5% and above 95% SoC — where a wider spread is normal (top-balancing near full, voltage sag near empty). Requires BYD Trip Stats Pro.
- **Tap-the-logo screenshot** *(Pro)* — tapping the **BYD logo** in the dashboard's top bar saves a PNG of the current screen to **Download/Screenshots/**, with a brief camera-style screen flash to confirm the capture. It saves the real composited frame (charts, gradients and the battery animation included), so you can quickly grab and share what's on screen. The flash plays only after the frame is captured, so it never appears in the saved image. Requires BYD Trip Stats Pro.
- **Battery health report** *(Pro)* — export a shareable summary of your battery's health from **Battery Degradation → Battery health report**, as a print-ready **PDF** or as **HTML** (opens in any browser). ### Changed

- **Battery degradation chart now hides the old under-reported SoH era automatically** — before v2.4.0 the app *calculated* State of Health in a way that read low, which left a misleading dip-and-recover in the trend (e.g. 98% → 95% → 98%). Trips recorded before that switch are now excluded from the chart, trend line, 80% projection and the battery health report by default, so the line reflects your battery's real history. A new **Battery data range** control (Battery Degradation → *Adjust data range*) lets you change this: it only affects what's shown and **never deletes any data**, every change can be undone, and re-including the old readings asks for confirmation first. (Replaces the previous, easy-to-mis-tap "data baseline" reset, which could wipe the chart's history view with a single tap.)
- **Battery Degradation summary cards** — the "Lowest recorded" card just mirrored "Current SoH" on a steadily degrading pack, so it's been replaced with **"Trips analysed"** (how many trips back the trend), giving four cards that each tell you something distinct. The battery health report does the same.
- **"Always On" is now the default background mode** — to keep parked telemetry, off-state (e.g. overnight) charging detection, and the Web Companion / MQTT working while the car is parked, the background service now defaults to **Always On** instead of Minimal. Any explicit mode you already chose is kept; only installs that never picked one change over. You can still switch to **Minimal** or **Deep Sleep** under Settings → *Background activity*.
- **Settings → Preferences reorganised** — the settings are now grouped under clear headings (**General**, **Trips**, **Battery & telemetry**, **Costs**, **Power & background**) with the **BYD Trip Stats Pro** card pinned at the top, instead of one long unsorted list. Units now sits next to Theme, trip settings are together, and so on — easier to scan as the list has grown.
- **Longer stops no longer split a drive into a new segment** — the live Distance metric shows `segment (cumulative)`, and a fresh segment used to begin after the car had been off for just 90 seconds. That window is now **5 minutes**, so a long red light, a traffic-control wait, or a brief stop in **P** keeps the drive as one continuous segment instead of starting a new one. (Stops where you stay in **D** never split the segment at all, regardless of length.) This is purely about the segment display — it doesn't change when a trip ends or the cumulative total.

### Fixed

- **Energy Consumption chart flatlining on longer trips** — both the overview and the detailed Energy chart could show a nearly flat line near zero on longer drives. The trip was being summarised down to a handful of points *before* the energy curve was calculated, and that calculation ignores gaps longer than a minute — so on a long trip almost every summarised interval was discarded and the curve never climbed. Energy is now integrated at full resolution first and the finished curve is summarised for display, so it reads correctly regardless of trip length, and the end-of-trip total is always kept.
- **Speed & Power overview charts smoothing away brief peaks and dips** — the condensed Speed and Power charts now keep both the highest and lowest value within each summarised slice, so a short full-throttle burst, a hard regen spike or a brief stop no longer disappears into a single averaged sample. Peaks also now sit at the moment they actually happened instead of drifting toward the middle of the slice.
- **Projected-range line drawn flat when you open the app part-way through a drive** — if the app wasn't open for the whole drive (you got in, drove, then opened it — or reopened after a back press), the projection curve was rebuilt by applying a single trip-average consumption rate to every past point. Since your charge barely moves over a short drive, that rendered as a near-flat line parallel to the axis, throwing away the shape the line would have had live. The curve is now rebuilt point-by-point using the consumption rate that was current *at each point* (the same rolling-window calculation the live chart uses), so it reflects how your driving actually varied — motorway vs town, climbs, regen — instead of flattening. As a bonus, when the app *was* open during the drive the exact live curve is reused verbatim (kept in memory, and on disk so it survives a full restart). Display-only — trip recording, distance, energy and the live projection math are unchanged.
- **Projected range collapsing to about half once the live estimate kicked in** — a few hundred metres into a drive (or the moment you opened the app mid-drive) the projected-range line could drop to roughly half and sit there, flatter and far below the car's own estimate, even while you were driving efficiently — e.g. a Seal Excellence cruising at 15.8 kWh/100 km projecting as if it were using nearly double that. The live estimate measured its energy with a per-tick counter that, now that telemetry arrives ~10×/second while the battery's discharge total only refreshes about once a second, was counting the in-between moments twice — inflating the consumption it projected from. It now reads energy from the same battery-discharge total the trip's energy and consumption figures already use, so the projected line matches your real consumption (and the rest of the curve) instead of halving. EVs and PHEVs both benefit; trip recording, distance and energy totals were already correct and are unchanged.
- **PHEV projected range reading far too high (e.g. 87 km at 19% with the BMS showing 10 km)** — the 2.8.0 change made the projection electric-only, but on plug-in hybrids it could still balloon to several times the real EV range. The projection divides the remaining battery energy by your measured electric consumption (Wh/km), and on a PHEV that consumption is deflated whenever the petrol engine is driving or topping up the battery: kilometres keep adding up while the traction battery barely drains, so the measured rate collapses far below anything the car can actually do on electricity, inflating the range. The electric consumption used for the projection is now floored at the car's rated figure, so it can't drop to a physically impossible value — the floor only takes effect in that engine-assisted regime, while genuine electric driving (including driving more efficiently *or* less efficiently than rated) still shows through. On larger-battery plug-in hybrids the projection also now uses the car's own reported remaining electric energy when available — near a low charge the battery holds back a reserve for the engine, so "capacity × charge %" overstated what's actually left for electric driving. BEVs are unaffected.

The trip card's gear letter and status text were updating on the once-a-second loop while the main gear indicator was already instant; they now share the same live gear.

The app now self-calibrates: if it ever sees those flags set *while driving* (which is physically impossible for a real charge), it stops trusting them on their own and requires actual charging power or a rising charge level to show charging. So a stuck flag no longer lights a phantom charging animation when you park, while genuine charging is still detected normally. Cars whose flags behave correctly are unaffected.
- **PHEV range projection always stuck at "≥ WLTP — capped at rated range"** — on plug-in hybrids the projected-range line added the petrol range on top of the electric projection, so with any fuel in the tank the total always blew past the electric WLTP ceiling and pinned at "capped," hiding the real comparison (e.g. a Sealion 6 DM-i at 65% and 19.6 kWh/100 km showed "≥ 92 km" instead of its true ~50 km electric range). The projection is now **electric-only** — it shows the EV range your actual consumption implies versus the BMS/WLTP estimate, matching what the SoC and consumption chart is about. BEVs are unaffected.
- **A new trip segment appearing mid-drive even though the engine never stopped** — a momentary gap in telemetry (a brief service hiccup while driving) was being mistaken for the car being switched off, splitting one continuous drive into two segments. A gap is now only treated as a car-off boundary when the odometer confirms the car genuinely wasn't moving across it.
- **Trip recording starting "from now" when the app is opened well into a drive** — opening the app 15–20 minutes into an already-moving trip started tracking (and live stats) from that moment instead of recovering the trip already in progress. The app now back-anchors to the in-progress journey on open, so distance and timing reflect the whole drive.
- **Wrong timestamps from an out-of-range ECU clock** — the head unit's reported datetime is now sanity-checked against the phone clock and ignored when it's implausibly far off, preventing skewed trip times.
- **Home Assistant entities stuck "Unavailable" despite MQTT data arriving** — after a WiFi drop the client reconnected internally and never re-published its "online" availability, so HA kept showing the last "offline" even while telemetry flowed into the broker. Availability is now re-published on every reconnect, and a failed publish forces a clean reconnect.
- **MQTT not publishing while parked in "Always On" mode** — in Always-On the parked poll loop had slowed to one tick every 5 minutes, so MQTT effectively went quiet between drives. Always-On now keeps a 30-second cadence while parked so telemetry keeps flowing to the broker.

- **Average speed showing 200+ km/h right after opening the app mid-drive** — the trip clock is now back-dated to match the recovered distance.
- **Avg speed/consumption briefly wrong after stopping then immediately restarting a trip** — the timing and off-state anchors now reset together at the moment you press Start.
- **Charging icon stuck "in progress" after a PHEV reaches 100%** — a misleading end-of-charge power blip is now ignored on cars that report proper charging power.
- **Trip history showing `SoC (BMS) 0% → 0%` on PHEVs** — trips and charging sessions now store panel SoC too, and all screens respect your selected source.
- **Front/rear motor RPM cards flickering between 0 and a real value** — a spurious 0 while moving now keeps the last known RPM.
- **Update banner not showing when upgrading from a beta to the stable release** — beta suffixes are now handled correctly in the version comparison.
- **Wrong distance/avg speed for auto-detected trips after a manual stop** — a fresh service start no longer back-anchors to a stale journey counter, which had inflated short trips by the day's earlier mileage.
- **Switching to Minimal while already parked never caught off-state charging** — the off-state keepalive (which wakes the app to sample overnight charging in Minimal) was only (re)armed on a car off→on→off cycle, not when you picked the mode in Settings. Coming from Deep Sleep — which cancels that keepalive — and switching to Minimal without driving left no keepalive scheduled at all, so plugging in the charger produced no telemetry. Selecting a mode now immediately reconciles background scheduling (arm the keepalive for Minimal, cancel it and go resident for Always On, cancel it for Deep Sleep). Valid between beta versions - released won't have this issue.
- **Wrong resume metrics after engine off/on within a trip** (Minimal/Deep Sleep) — the trip now re-syncs from the database on resume so distance, energy, and consumption stay continuous.
- **Live segment distance frozen at "0.0" after a brief stop and restart** — the live Distance was computed purely from the odometer, which the car's mileage ECU reports late for several seconds after an engine restart, so the new segment stayed at 0 even while moving. The speed-integrated distance (which was being calculated but never shown) now bridges that gap, so Distance keeps moving the instant any speed reading is available and snaps back to the odometer once it ticks.
- **Speed frozen at 0 while driving (poll-only getter going stale)** — the dashboard speed came solely from polling the BYD `getCurrentSpeed` getter, which on some firmwares freezes at a stale 0 after an auto-started trip and only recovers on an engine off/on. The speed device's live **event stream** (the same mechanism that keeps motor RPM updating) was being received but only logged, never applied. Speed is now driven from that event stream — the app auto-learns the event-to-km/h scale while the getter still works, then keeps the speedometer alive from events when the getter stalls, falling back to the getter when events aren't flowing.
- **Gear stuck on "P" while driving** — on firmwares where the gearbox device never reports a gear (its `gearCode`/`currentGear` getters return NULL and no gear event fires), the gear stayed pinned to its default "P" even at speed, because the code trusted the gearbox source unconditionally and never fell back to the speed-based guess. Since gear=D/R is also used as a "car on" proxy, this could knock other readings off too. A physical-impossibility guard now overrides "P" to "D" whenever the car is clearly moving forward (>5 km/h), and a diagnostic was added to pinpoint whether the gearbox device is silent or actively misreporting.
- **Speed flickering to 0 mid-drive from the event stream overriding a healthy getter** — on firmwares where the speed event's raw value isn't proportional to km/h, the learned scale was meaningless and the event-derived value (which could land anywhere, including ~0) was overriding a perfectly good `getCurrentSpeed` reading whenever an event had arrived in the last 3 s — i.e. constantly while driving. The polled getter is now authoritative whenever it's healthy; the event stream only bridges a genuinely frozen getter, and only when GPS or traction power independently confirm the car is moving (so it can't invent speed at a standstill either).
- **Energy consumption over-reported (regen not credited)** — the per-tick energy accumulator summed only *positive* changes in the BYD consumption counter, which silently discarded every regen tick and reported **gross** energy instead of **net**. On trips with meaningful regen (e.g. a long descent) this over-stated consumption substantially. The accumulator now sums both signs (discharge minus regen) for true net consumption, while still dropping the counter's car-on reset so multi-segment trips stay correct. The stored trip energy also no longer prefers the running *peak* discharge (which sits above the end value once you regen past it).

The trip-controls card now resets distance / time / segment immediately at the press moment so the AVG starts from zero and climbs to the real value as the trip accumulates, instead of inheriting the pre-trip back-anchored journey distance divided by a brand-new elapsed timer.

PM2.5 is now re-polled from the climate device every 5 seconds so the debounce can confirm.
- **Range metric showing 0 or the fuel driving range on PHEVs** — the range projection used `soc` (BMS) to compute remaining energy; when BMS SoC briefly reads 0 at engine wake-up the projection collapsed to 0 or, on PHEVs, snapped to the fuel-only range (e.g. 900 km). SoC now falls back to the panel reading when BMS reports 0. Additionally, the Range stat card on PHEVs now shows the car's own EV-only range estimate instead of the trip projection (which included the fuel offset and produced misleadingly large numbers).

### Fixed

- **Live "energy consumed" no longer spikes to thousands of kWh at trip start** — and now stays in step with the trip overview's stored value (which is what reference apps like Electro report for the same drive). The live Trip Tracking card reads the BMS `totalDischarge` counter delta against the *corrected* trip-start anchor exposed by `TripRepository.currentTripStartTotalDischarge()`. The repository runs a per-tick self-correction (see [TripRepository.kt](app/src/main/java/com/byd/tripstats/data/repository/TripRepository.kt)) the moment the BMS produces a plausible reading after a stale `0` at trip start, so the live delta picks up the corrected anchor automatically instead of carrying the multi-thousand-kWh spike forward. As defence-in-depth for the brief window before the correction fires (or if the override path supplies an unsane override), the displayed value is clamped to the physical envelope `[0, 2 × pack capacity]` and falls back to the power-integrated traction estimate outside it.
- **Range projection now reports a realistic value from the first kilometre instead of being pinned in `● Baseline`** — on Seal Excellence (and any car with a sparse or unreliable engine-power signal) the projection used to sit on the catalog baseline for the entire drive, sometimes underestimating range by half. Several things changed together: (a) the rolling-window energy accumulator and the speed-bin accumulator both feed from the BMS `totalDischarge` delta — the same source the CONS readout uses — with engine-power only as a fallback, both during a live drive and when restoring an in-progress trip from disk; (b) the speed-bin accumulator counts distance every tick, with energy added only when positive, so sparse BMS updates can't starve the bin distance below `BIN_MIN_DIST_KM`; (c) the `● Historical bins` tier averages Wh/km across *all* populated speed bins weighted by distance, and — crucially — falls back to the trip-cumulative consumption (the exact figure the CONS readout shows: trip-total kWh ÷ trip distance) whenever the speed bins themselves haven't accumulated usable energy. That trip-cumulative path always resolves once the drive has covered ≥ 0.2 km and consumed anything, so the projection leaves the catalog baseline regardless of how erratically the firmware increments its discharge counter; (d) the model badge is picked across all three tiers (`● Live trip` / `● Speed bins` / `● Baseline`) when the app is reopened mid-trip instead of skipping `● Speed bins` entirely; (e) the orange projection line is drawn from km 0 in the restored chart rather than leaving a 3 km gap before the old stabilisation threshold. End result on a typical city drive: the badge flips from `● Baseline` to `● Speed bins` within ~0.2 km, then to `● Live trip` at ~3 km, and the projection converges to roughly `(remaining kWh × 100) / CONS` from the first kilometre instead of after 30+ km of driving.
- **Range projection chart polish** — the projected orange line now starts at km 0 instead of waiting for the first stabilised point (was a small but visible gap near the y-axis); the y-axis stays well-formed on rare cold-boot ticks where the BMS hasn't reported a range yet at trip start (could previously degenerate to a zero-height chart); and saturated projections (above the WLTP cap, i.e. calibration not yet settled) now render as a soft asymptotic bulge into the top padding rather than a hard line clipped at the chart's ceiling, so they're visible regardless of starting SoC.
- **Live Distance metric now shows the new segment alongside cumulative trip distance after an engine-off resume** — e.g. `13.3 (17.8)` once you've driven 13.3 km in the post-resume segment of a 17.8 km trip. Previously the segment anchor was set from the BYD `currentJourneyDriveMileage` counter, which on some firmwares doesn't reset across a brief engine-off cycle — so segment ended up equal to cumulative and the parentheses never appeared. The off→on transition handler now anchors the segment at the current odometer directly, and the same correction is applied on app-restart restore by scanning the stored data points for the most recent off-state gap.
- **Range projection chart now colours each segment by its own projected-vs-BMS verdict** — the orange/green line and shaded area used to take a single colour based only on where the *current* projection sat relative to BMS, repainting the entire trip history in that colour. Each segment between consecutive sampled points is now classified independently: above the BMS line it renders green (you outperformed expectation across that stretch), below it renders amber, with the shaded area beneath the line matching segment by segment. The duplicate "Δ" label that used to float at the leading edge inside the chart is gone too — only the chip in the top-right of the chart frame and the existing vertical tape-measure line + tick caps remain as the delta indicator.
- **Stored trip energy is now resistant to a stale `totalDischarge` anchor** — `TripRepository` now (a) re-anchors a trip's `startTotalDischarge` on the fly the moment the BMS produces a reading that implies the original anchor was a stale zero (observed delta exceeds both 2 × pack capacity and the power-integrated estimate of energy actually drawn so far), back-computing the corrected anchor from the integrated draw, (b) clamps the final `(endTotalDischarge − startTotalDischarge)` to the power-integrated estimate at trip-close time if the reported delta is still physically impossible, and (c) applies the same plausibility clamp at each 30 s segment flush, using a per-segment slice of the trip's power-integrated draw as the fallback (a 30 s segment can't physically consume more than ~2 kWh; anything above 50% of pack capacity is unambiguously corrupt). Together these protect the stored `energyConsumed`, the per-segment `energyUsed`, and every downstream chart, consumption stat, monthly total, and seasonal analysis that depends on them against the same stale-zero failure mode that was corrupting the live display.
- **Battery temperature now shows a consistent, accurate value across all screens** — the Dashboard stat card, Trip Detail average, and Trip Detail min/max range now all agree with each other and reflect actual battery cell temperature (not a coolant-side probe that reads several °C lower).
- **Battery temperature decoder rewritten to match BYD's actual encoding** — earlier 2.4.1 builds tried to guess between two possible encodings (direct °C vs ÷3) using anchors and ambient as tiebreakers, and got it consistently wrong by a few degrees against reference apps (Electro, the open-source Overdrive). The decoder now uses BYD's documented `raw − 40 = °C` formula directly, with raw values validated to be in `0..120` (i.e. −40°C to 80°C). Result: cell temperatures match what every other BYD-aware app on your car reports, instead of being 4–5°C off in either direction.
- **Battery cell temp sentinels no longer clobber the last good reading** — the BMS-uninitialized value 0 and BYD's INVALID/UNAVAILABLE sentinels are filtered out so quiet polls don't overwrite a real temperature with a placeholder.
- **Old trips with bad stored temperature averages now display sensibly** — for trips recorded before this fix, the average battery temperature shown in Trip Detail is now re-derived from the min/max cell range rather than the previously stored (potentially wrong) average.
- **Battery temperature max is now shown in Trip Detail even when the car doesn't report it directly** — the max is estimated from the min and average, so you'll see a range like "20°C – 22°C" instead of just "20°C".
- **Trip export dialog sections are now collapsible** — "Save to Download folder" and "Send to Telegram bot" each have a tappable header with a chevron and start collapsed. The dialog now opens with just the Clipboard button and two section headers, so users can see the two groups exist at a glance and tap into whichever they want, instead of being faced with seven buttons up-front. The previously hidden third Telegram button ("Send HTML viewer to Telegram") is now discoverable inside the expanded section.
- **Trip duration and average speed no longer count engine-off resume time** — when you stop the engine and the trip stays open inside the configurable engine-off resume window (Settings → Preferences), the wallclock keeps ticking. Until now that time was getting added into the saved trip's duration, which made average speed read low (e.g. a 30 km trip you actually drove in 45 min would average ~20 km/h instead of ~40 km/h if the trip closed 45 min after the engine went off because the resume window was 30 min and you parked overnight). The stored duration and avg speed now reflect actual driving time only — gaps in the recorded data points where the car was off are subtracted. Existing trips are back-filled automatically on first launch after the upgrade (one-shot pass over every completed trip), and the live Trip Tracking card's TIME / AVG also subtract any mid-trip engine-off windows in real time. Schema bumped v3 → v4 to add the per-trip stored value.
- **Gear indicator no longer says "P / Trip in progress" when the car auto-parks at a stop** — BYD automatically shifts to P at a standstill (e.g. in traffic), which previously caused the gear circle to read "P" and the status to read "Trip in progress" — misleading, since the user hasn't actually parked. The gear circle now shows "·" in a muted colour and the status reads "Stopped" while a trip is active and the car has auto-shifted to P. A manual P (no active trip) still displays as "P" and "Waiting for Trip…".
- **Charging session no longer stays "in progress" after you unplug and drive away** — some firmwares don't fire the unplug callback when the plug is removed while the car is locked (most common: charge overnight, walk up in the morning, unplug, drive off). The plug-presence flag stayed stuck non-zero, so the app kept showing "charging" through the next drive and the overnight session in history stayed open. The "is charging now" signal now uses the BMS-level charger work state (which clears once the BMS actually finishes a session) as its primary input, with a defence-in-depth override that force-clears the charging state the moment the car is genuinely driving (speed > 5 km/h in D or R) — physically impossible to be charging at that point. On install, any session left open from before the fix is auto-closed on the next trip start with its end-of-data-points timestamp and final SoC.
- **MCU WiFi keepalive during charging now triggers on all three charging signals** — to prevent the car cutting WiFi ~15 minutes after ACC_OFF mid-charge, the periodic keepalive ping now fires whenever the BMS reports active charging, an active charger work state, *or* the plug being physically in the port. Earlier 2.4.1 builds used only the plug-presence signal; if the plug callback misfired, the keepalive missed and the app could lose telemetry mid-charge.
- **Telegram auto-backup no longer wastes data on oversized DBs** — the periodic Telegram backup worker (and any manual "Send to Telegram" tap) used to upload the entire database file before the Telegram Bot API rejected it with "Request Entity Too Large" — Telegram bots cap at 50 MB. A heavy user with a 100 MB DB on a daily schedule could push close to 500 MB of wasted traffic over five days. The worker now checks the DB size *before* the multipart upload starts, skips the run when the DB exceeds 50 MB, and surfaces a clear message ("Backup file is X MB — Telegram bots can only accept files up to 50 MB. Upload skipped to avoid wasting your data plan."). The local-backup path is unaffected.
- **ABRP uploads now pause when the car is parked and not charging** — previously the ABRP integration fired its upload loop from the foreground telemetry service whenever it was alive, which (with "Keep service alive when car is off" defaulting to true since 2.4.0) meant uploads continued 24/7 even though nothing meaningful about the car was changing. ABRP cares only about driving/charging telemetry, so the new gate (`!isCarOn && !isCharging` → skip) removes ~80% of the requests without losing any data that ABRP would actually use.
- **ABRP no longer reopens a fresh TLS connection on every upload** — `HttpURLConnection.disconnect()` was being called after every request, which forced the underlying TCP/TLS socket closed and disabled Android's HTTP connection-pool reuse. With ~17 k requests/day in the worst case, that was paying a ~5–8 KB TLS handshake on every single one. The success path now drains the response body and lets the pool keep the socket alive; disconnect still runs on the IOException path so a half-broken connection doesn't poison the pool. Saves ~9× bytes per request once the connection is warm.

> **What's new in a nutshell**

The biggest change is that the app now stays connected by default while the car is off — this means the battery history chart no longer has gaps, and charging sessions that happen while you're away from the car are now captured properly (including real-time data points and correct kWh totals). Both the live display and the finalised trip now use the SDK main odometer delta as the sole source of truth; `journeyMileage` and speed integration are retained only as fallbacks for the edge case where no odometer anchor is available (e.g. app connects mid-journey on a cold boot).

The service now also schedules the keepalive directly from inside its self-stop path — by the time `carOff && !charging` for 5 minutes has been observed from real telemetry, we are independently certain the car is off and don't need a broadcast to confirm it. This ensures the service correctly identifies an off-state charging session, switches to 30 s polling, and records real-time data points throughout rather than falling back to 5-minute ticks.

- **rawJson no longer duplicates dedicated column data** — 25 fields already persisted in typed DB columns (soc, speed, gear, latitude, longitude, altitude, tyre pressures, tyre temperatures, cell voltages, battery total voltage, 12V voltage, soh, engine speeds, electric range, soc panel) have been removed from the per-point JSON blob. New data points are ~700 bytes smaller per row (~33% reduction). Fields that have no dedicated column (drive\_mode, regen\_mode, car\_on, statistic\_\*, instrument temps, bodywork diagnostics, etc.) are retained.

### Fixed

- **Battery cell temperature from statistic device auto-detects the encoding** — the BYD SDK returns `cellTAvg` / `cellTMin` / `cellTMax` in one of two encodings depending on firmware: direct °C on some firmwares, 1/3 °C units on others. Applying the wrong interpretation produced either an underread (e.g., 11.5°C when ambient was 32°C) or an overread (e.g., 62°C when ambient was 25°C). The fix uses the in-car ambient temperature as a reference and picks the interpretation whose deviation from ambient is physically plausible (within ~30°C parked/driving, ~50°C during DC fast charging).
- **Battery temperature sentinels suppressed when ambient is unavailable** — `resolveStatisticTempCelsius` was returning the divided value (raw ÷ 3) even when no ambient reference was available to validate it. Firmware sentinels like 186 (→ 186÷3=62°C) passed through undetected if the instrument device had not yet reported outdoor temperature. The function now returns null until ambient is known.
- **Service self-stop tripwire no longer fires on fresh start** — `lastFeatureEventElapsedMs` defaults to 0, which previously made the "SDK silent for 10 minutes" check evaluate to true immediately on first launch (any uptime exceeded the threshold before any SDK events arrived). The service would self-stop before SDK devices could register, blocking all telemetry until the user physically drove the car. The tripwire now requires at least one real SDK event before the silence threshold is evaluated.
- **Off-state idle no longer thrashes the service overnight** — three independent periodic restart sources (`ServiceWatchdogWorker` 15-min, `ServiceRestarterJobService` periodic 15-min, and `ServiceRestartReceiver` boot-action kicks) were re-starting the telemetry service throughout the night, undoing the carOff+notCharging self-stop and keeping the wake lock + WiFi lock held for ~33% of the overnight period. A new persistent `ServiceIdleState` flag is set when the service self-stops; every periodic restart source now reads the flag and skips the restart while idle. `BootReceiver` no longer re-arms restart kicks on `ACC_OFF` / `POWER_DISCONNECTED`. The off-state wake-lock duty cycle drops from ~33% to ~5%, eliminating the overnight 12V drain.
- **Service idle self-stop now truly stops the service** — the previous implementation called `stopSelf()` but did not set `intentionalStop = true`, causing `onDestroy` to immediately reschedule a restart via `scheduleSelfRestart`. The service was effectively cycling: stop → restart → 5 min → stop → restart, running all night and continuously publishing MQTT snapshots. Setting `intentionalStop = true` before `stopSelf()` breaks the loop; the service stays stopped until the next 90-minute alarm fires intentionally.
- **MQTT idle publish cadence corrected** — as a consequence of the above fix, MQTT now publishes a single snapshot every ~90 minutes while the car is off and idle (from the `OffStateKeepaliveReceiver` alarm), rather than every 5 minutes from the unintended restart loop. The MQTT settings hint has been updated to reflect this: "Idle: snapshot every ~90 min".
- **BYD SDK temperature sentinel values unconditionally rejected** — `resolveStatisticTempCelsius` now rejects raw values of 195 (`TEMPERATURE_INVALID`) and -60 (`TEMPERATURE_MIN`) before any ambient comparison logic. Previously these sentinel values could pass through when no ambient reference was yet available, producing spurious readings such as 65°C (195 ÷ 3) on the Seal Excellence firmware.
- **Live trip tracking card consumption matches finalized trip stats** — the energy counter in the trip tracking card now uses the battery discharge counter delta (`currentTotalDischarge − sessionStartTotalDischarge`), the same source used by finalized trip statistics. The previous motor power integration approach excluded AC, 12V auxiliary, and other non-motor loads, causing a systematic under-read of 10–20% when climate control was active.
- **Live trip tracking card average speed matches finalized trip stats** — average speed is now derived from the pure odometer delta (`currentOdometer − sessionStartOdometer`), matching the `endOdometer − startOdometer` formula used by finalized trips. The integration-based distance estimate is retained as a fallback only when the odometer has not yet advanced.
- **Backup deletion for "Download (file)" entries now works on Android 10+** — backups created by older app versions that wrote directly to the filesystem had no MediaStore record, so `File.delete()` and MediaStore-by-name lookups both silently failed. The deletion logic now follows a 4-step escalation: direct `File.delete()` → MediaStore lookup by file path (`DATA` column) → MediaStore lookup by display name → insert the file into MediaStore to gain ownership, then delete via the returned content URI.

### Maintenance

- **User-triggered "Trim database" action** in the Local Backup screen (new "Maintenance" section). Replaces the earlier auto-compaction-on-startup approach so the user runs it at their convenience and gets explicit feedback. Five phases run sequentially with live progress:
  - **A. Strip redundant rawJson fields** from every row in `trip_data_points` and `charging_data_points` (the 25 keys already persisted as typed columns).
  - **B. Clear rawJson** to `{}` for trip and charging points older than **45 days** — the diagnostic payload is no longer needed for historical data and recovers the largest amount of space.
  - **C. Downsample old trip points to 1 row per minute** for trips older than **60 days**. The native sampling rate of ~1 row per 30 s is preserved for recent trips; older trips keep `MIN(id)` per `(tripId, minute)` bucket. Distance, energy, and time-series shape remain accurate; chart smoothness is slightly reduced for very old trips.
  - **D. Delete charging data points for AC sessions older than 45 days.** AC vs DC is inferred from `charging_sessions.peakKw < 25 kW`; DC fast-charging sessions are kept entirely (rare and interesting to review). The session summary row (kWh added, peak kW, duration, etc.) is preserved for both.
  - **E. VACUUM via raw SQLite** — the telemetry service is briefly stopped, Room is closed, and `PRAGMA wal_checkpoint(TRUNCATE)` + `VACUUM` are run on a direct SQLite connection (the database file would otherwise stay locked by Room's connection pool, leaving freed pages unreclaimed). The app then auto-restarts so Room reopens cleanly — same pattern used after a database restore.
  - The result and timestamp are persisted across app restarts; the section header always shows "Last trimmed: …" with a one-line summary of the rows affected. Logged with tag `DatabaseTrimmer`. Expected to recover the bulk of the 500 MB+ accumulation since v2.1.0.

- **14 Heatmaps** — four new heatmaps added: Tyre Pressure vs Consumption, SoC vs Regen Efficiency, Speed vs Battery Temperature, Cell Voltage Spread vs SoC. The Cell Voltage Spread chart uses a widened y-tick column (70 dp) to prevent label overlap with the rotated axis label.

### Changed

Combined with "Retain published values" in Electro, this ensures the broker delivers the latest state immediately on reconnect rather than waiting for the next publish interval.
